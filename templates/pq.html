Use of PQ trees to get uncertainties and candidate orderings for noisy data

This method uses a data structure called PQ tree to estimate the uncertainties in the dataset if the data is noisy. If the ordered graph constructed using the MST approach has non-uniform branch distribution and a high noise to signal ratio, use this method to get permutations of probable orderings of data samples. Permutations are due to the nature of interior nodes of the tree. The P-nodes have a minimum of two children with relaxed ordering, while the Q-nodes have a minimum of three children with only strict reversal of child nodes.
